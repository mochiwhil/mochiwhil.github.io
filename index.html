    My First Try

    This is the first time I have used a digital drawing board to complete a poster.

    It was a poster designed for a school Winter Olympics themed sports day, each year level needed to introduce a Winter Olympics event and we chose figure skating. I used an *S-shape* composition to separate the text from the images on the one hand. On the other hand, the *S-shape* makes the picture more dynamic and fits in with the theme of the sport 'figure skating'. The blue and pink colour scheme suggests snow sports on the one hand, and the softness of figure skating on the other. In this attempt, I used procreate for the first time and I continued to practice editing text, a basic function. I was able to import new fonts and edit text forms. I also learnt to use vector graphics and brush strokes to decorate the images in the posters as elements.

    #First try on Propaganda Board

    A guide to learning methods was requested as a theme for the school. I used an innovative e-picture board method, printing out each section on a large piece of sea newspaper for a more structured whole.

    However, there were many shortcomings to this first job. For example, the colours were over-saturated and the printout was dark. The fonts were too small and the typography was a bit messy. The poster paper is a soft material and can be easily torn by the wind or wet by rain when attached to an open-air board.
